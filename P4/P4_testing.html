About the Client

Cattius is a 67 years old lady that loves cats. She is a retired veterinarian that is altruistic in nature. During her career, she worked for 40 years in an animal shelter, mostly focused on domestic animals. Cattius wishes to remain active within the community even though retired, and feels that our website may help her achieve her goal of bringing animals, specifically cats, and people together.

Testing Document

To test our website, we did a real life testing plus send out a survey via google forms.

Google Form

To test the website we sent out this survey.
The survey collect data about the tester and devices. To make sure that the tester is going around the whole website, they are asked to find special information on the site. In the end the test person is asked about the navigation structure and the appearance of the whole website.

Real life testing

We also did a real life testing. Here we had one test user trying to find the same information as in the survey. We observed that the test user quickly figured out the navigation between the different pages. though there was a problem when she tried to push the first "label" on the navigation bar, e.g. the adopt, which does not have a link.

Tester Audience

Even though we wanted to reach the intended users for the website, the people in the age from 50 and up, the survey was sent out to our acquaintances, who basically is students. This means that the feedback we got might be a little of compared to the feedback we would get from the intended users.

Feedback summary

The feedback we received can be found here.

  1. Navigation bar
    • Our test users found it annoying that they could not press the first element in the bar. The adopt and the about buttons, which both have the dropdown menus.
  2. Case 4
    • Finding information about what to do if the test users wants to visit the Cat Castle. Here had 8/37 answered that they could not find this information. Which we also discovered with the life testing. Instead of clicking contact, where the address is. The users try home or about us.
  3. Navigation around the page
    • The test users found it easy to navigate around the website, because of the navigation bar.
  4. Appearance
    • It was not all the test users, that found the appearance of the website appealing. But they agreed that the theme matches the purpose, which is the important thing.
  5. Cat gallery
    • We got a comment concerning our cat gallery, that we should make the visitor switch between the different cat manually.

Your Response

  1. Navigation bar
    • Here we could have implemented a link on the top button. And then when you click it, you will get to first page of the ones in the dropdown.
  2. Case 4
    • We could implement the address in the footer, then it is always visible.
  3. Navigation around the page
    • The navigation bar was used as intended
  4. Appearance
    • The theme of the website was designed to match the atmosphere of the Cat Castle
  5. Cat gallery
    • It would be nice to make the gallery more user involving by giving the visitor the opportunity to click from cat to cat. This could be done in the JavaScript with a counter.

Self-reflection

When looking back on this project, our group can see that we have learned a lot. First of all, building structure of a website (navbar etc.) is more demanding and time consuming than we thought. Since everything else is dependent on the structure, it is important to complete the structure early on in the project. However, since there were quite a lot of issues with the structure, all other deadlines had to be postponed.

We have also learned that a project like this demands team effort. Not all members are as motivated as others. This was a problem since not all members had the same goals and work attitudes for the project. This resulted in some uneven distribution of workload among the members of the group. Also, each member has a different set of strengths and weaknesses, which had both positive and negative effects on our project.

The next important lesson we learned is that communication is key! Since none of the members of this group study the same program, it is difficult to arrange meetings. With two exchange students and three Norwegian students who study different fields, this was certainly an issue. However, in the end we managed to complete our requirements for the project.

We also learned that planning the design is a working progress. It was difficult to know in the beginning which functions could be implemented in which languages. For example, the navbar was planned to implement in JavaScript, but as the course progressed and our knowledge increased, we realized that the navbar could easily be created in HTML and CSS. Therefore, we had to change our plan of the design during the course of the project.

Finally, we learned that sometimes, when you're stuck on a problem, it is best to take a step back and try again later or the next day. Sometimes the brain just needs to get some rest and distance from the problem.
